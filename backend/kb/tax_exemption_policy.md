# Tax Exemption Policy

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-15
**Applies To:** All AcmeCo Industrial accounts

---

## Overview

AcmeCo Industrial supports tax-exempt purchasing for qualifying organisations. Tax exemption is applied at the account level and covers all orders placed under that account once a valid certificate is on file and approved.

---

## Eligibility

The following entity types may qualify for tax-exempt status:
- Government agencies (federal, state, municipal)
- Registered non-profit organisations (501(c)(3) and similar)
- Manufacturers purchasing goods for resale or direct use in production
- Agricultural businesses purchasing industrial gases for qualifying farm use
- Educational institutions

---

## How to Submit a Tax Exemption Certificate

1. **Obtain the correct certificate** for your state. Common forms include:
   - ST-105 (Indiana), ST-120 (New York), CRT-61 (Illinois), MTC Multijurisdictional Certificate
2. **Email your completed certificate** to **tax@acmeco.com** with the subject line: `Tax Exempt Application – [Your Account ID]`
3. **Include your Account ID** (format: ACC-XXXX) in the email body. Your Account ID appears on all invoices.
4. Our tax team will review and respond within **3 business days**.
5. Once approved, exemption is applied to all future orders. It cannot be applied retroactively to past invoices.

---

## Certificate Requirements

- Certificate must be fully completed — unsigned or incomplete forms will be rejected
- Certificate must be issued for the correct state(s) where deliveries occur
- Certificates must not be expired
- If your organisation operates in multiple states, a separate certificate is required per state

---

## Account-Level vs Order-Level Exemption

Tax exemption at AcmeCo is **account-level**, not order-level. This means:
- Once approved, exemption applies automatically to all orders on the account
- You do not need to submit a certificate per order
- If you have locations in multiple states, each state's certificate must be on file before exemption applies in that state

---

## Certificate Expiry and Renewal

- AcmeCo will send a reminder **60 days before** your certificate expires
- Renewed certificates should be submitted via the same process above
- If a certificate lapses and a taxable order ships before renewal, AcmeCo cannot issue a retroactive tax credit — renewal must be completed before the order ships
- You can check your current certificate expiry status by contacting your Account Manager or emailing tax@acmeco.com

---

## Checking Your Tax-Exempt Status

To verify whether your account currently has tax-exempt status:
- Contact your Account Manager directly
- Email tax@acmeco.com with your Account ID
- Check your most recent invoice — tax-exempt accounts show $0.00 in the tax line

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: My account was charged tax on a recent order but we are tax-exempt. What do I do?**
A: Email tax@acmeco.com with your Account ID and invoice number. If the certificate was on file and valid at the time of the order, AcmeCo will issue a tax credit on your next invoice within 5 business days.

**Q: We recently received tax-exempt status. Can it be applied to orders already placed?**
A: No. Exemption applies from the approval date forward only.

**Q: Our exemption cert covers multiple states. Do I need to submit one form?**
A: If using an MTC Multijurisdictional Certificate, a single submission covers all participating states. State-specific forms require one submission per state.

**Q: How long does the review process take?**
A: Standard review is 3 business days. Complex cases (multi-state, non-standard entity types) may take up to 5 business days.
