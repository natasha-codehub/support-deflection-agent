# Refund and Short Pay Dispute Process

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-01
**Applies To:** All AcmeCo Industrial accounts

---

## Overview

A **short pay** occurs when a customer receives fewer units than the quantity billed on the invoice. AcmeCo Industrial has a formal dispute process to investigate and resolve these discrepancies. Most disputes are resolved within 5 business days.

---

## What Is a Short Pay?

A short pay is a billing discrepancy where:
- The **invoice quantity** is greater than the **delivered quantity**, or
- A product is billed but not received at all

Short pays are distinct from product damage claims or delivery disputes (e.g., wrong item delivered). If you received the correct quantity but the product was damaged, contact your Account Manager for a separate damage claim process.

---

## How to Raise a Short Pay Dispute

### Step 1 — Gather Documentation

Before submitting, collect:
- **Invoice number** (format: INV-XXXX) — found on your invoice email or invoice PDF
- **Order ID** (format: ORD-XXXX) — found on your delivery note or order confirmation
- **Received quantity** — the actual count at the delivery location
- **Signed delivery note or receiving log** (if available — not mandatory but speeds resolution)

### Step 2 — Submit the Dispute

Email **disputes@acmeco.com** with:
- Subject line: `Short Pay Dispute – [Invoice Number]`
- Body must include: Account ID, Invoice Number, Order ID, billed quantity, received quantity, and delivery location
- Attach any supporting documentation (photos, signed receiving logs)

Alternatively, ask your Account Manager to log the dispute on your behalf.

### Step 3 — Investigation and Resolution

- AcmeCo will acknowledge your dispute within **1 business day**
- Our logistics team will cross-check the dispatch manifest, driver route log, and any available delivery photos
- Resolution is issued within **5 business days** of acknowledgement
- If the short pay is confirmed, AcmeCo will issue a **credit note** against the disputed invoice amount
- The credit note will appear on your next invoice as a line-item deduction

---

## Refund Policy

AcmeCo issues **credit notes**, not cash refunds, for confirmed short pay disputes. Credit notes are applied to future invoices automatically.

Exceptions where a cash refund may be issued:
- Account closure
- Overpayment on a prepaid order
- Disputes resolved after account closure

To request a cash refund in an eligible case, email **ar@acmeco.com** with your Account ID and reference number.

## SLA and Escalation

| Stage | SLA |
|---|---|
| Dispute acknowledgement | 1 business day |
| Resolution | 5 business days |
| Credit note issuance | 2 business days after resolution |

If your dispute has not been resolved within 5 business days, escalate to your Account Manager who can prioritise the case with the disputes team.

---

## Disputed Quantity Threshold

- Disputes for **1 unit or less** on orders of 20+ units may be resolved at Account Manager discretion without formal investigation, particularly for long-standing accounts
- Disputes claiming **more than 50% of the billed quantity** were not received require additional documentation (signed receiving log mandatory)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I received 8 cylinders but was billed for 10. What do I do?**
A: Email disputes@acmeco.com with your invoice number (INV-XXXX) and order ID (ORD-XXXX), stating you received 8 and were billed for 10. Include your delivery location. We will investigate and resolve within 5 business days.

**Q: How do I find my invoice number?**
A: Your invoice number appears in the subject line of your invoice email (format: INV-XXXX) and on the top-right of your PDF invoice.

**Q: Can I withhold payment while a dispute is open?**
A: You may short-pay the invoice by the disputed amount while the dispute is open without incurring late payment penalties, as long as the dispute has been formally submitted.

**Q: What if the driver says they delivered the full quantity?**
A: If there is a discrepancy between driver records and your receiving log, AcmeCo will review the dispatch manifest and any available delivery photos. Our disputes team makes the final determination based on all available evidence.

**Q: How long do I have to raise a dispute?**
A: Disputes must be raised within **30 days** of the invoice date. Disputes raised after 30 days may not be eligible for credit.
